How to Perform Bhujangasana

  • Lie flat on your stomach with your legs extended and toes pointing back.
  • Place your hands under your shoulders, elbows close to your body.
  • Press into your palms and lift your chest off the floor, straightening your arms partially or fully.
  • Keep your shoulders relaxed and down, away from your ears.
  • Look slightly upward while maintaining length in your neck.
  • Hold the pose for several breaths and lower back down slowly.

Health Benefits of Bhujangasana

Strengthens the spine and improves posture

Regular practice builds upper body strength and stability.

Strengthens the thighs, knees, and ankles

Lift arms overhead, open chest, and draw shoulder blades back.

Stimulates abdominal organs and aids digestion

Twist your torso gently, engaging your core to massage abdominal organs.

Alleviates back pain and sciatica

Try gentle backbends or seated forward folds to decompress the spine.

Opens heart and lungs, improving breathing

Open arms wide, lift chest, and take deep breaths to expand lungs.
